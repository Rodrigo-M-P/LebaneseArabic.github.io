📘 Lebanese Arabic Rulebook — Comprehensive, Easy-to-Follow

Scope: Practical rules for Lebanese (Levantine) Arabic, not Classical/Standard. Transliteration: 2=ʔ, 3=ʕ, 5=kh, 7=ḥ, gh=غ, sh=ش. Goal: sound clear and natural.

1) Sound System & Pronunciation

  • qāf ق → 2 (glottal stop ʔ) in urban speech: 2alb (heart), su2āl (question). Retention of q (some rural) or g occurs and is understood.
  • j ج → zh /ʒ/: jamīl → zhamīl, jayy → zhayy (coming).
  • th ث → s, dh ذ → z, ẓ ظ → z (often emphatic): thalāthe → tlete/slete, dhahab → zahab.
  • 5 خ = /x/, gh غ = /ɣ/ are robust: 5obz (bread), ghali (expensive).
  • sh ش = sh; Lebanese sometimes write ch (French influence) but pronounce sh.
  • r ر tap/trill; geminated rr is longer.
  • Article assimilation: l shams → sh‑shams (see §4.2).

Vowels & Stress

  • Vowels: /a e i o u/; feminine ة pronounced -e: sayyāre.
  • Imāla (a→e near y/j) is common: dayfa → deife.
  • Stress: heavy last syllable → stress last; otherwise penult. Clitics usually don’t attract stress.

2) Writing & Transliteration

  • Numbers: 2=ʔ, 3=ʕ, 5=kh, 7=ḥ; gh=غ, sh=ش.
  • Use -e for feminine ending (ة). Drop vowels in clusters: tlete → tlet, sette → sett.
  • Write 2 for qāf unless you purposely mark q.

3) Nouns, Gender, Plurals, Agreement

  • Gender: masculine (default), feminine -e. Adjectives agree: bayt kbiir (m), sayyāre kbiire (f).
  • Definiteness: article l‑ on noun + adjective: l‑bayt l‑kbiir.
  • Plurals: sound m -iin, f -aat; many broken plurals: walad → wled, bayt → byūt.
  • Dual (colloquial): say -en (m) / -ten (f): kalben, jome3ten.

4) Determiners, Demonstratives, Article

  • Demonstratives: near → hayda (m), hayde (f), hol/hawde (pl); far → haydak, haydik, holik/hawlik.
  • Article l‑ with assimilation: before sun letters l assimilates: l shams → sh‑shams; before moon letters: pronounced normally: l 2amar.
  • Idāfe (N1 of N2): bayt Tony; with pronouns/clarity, use taba3: taba3e, taba3ak…

5) Pronouns & Clitics

Pronouns: ana, enta, ente, ento, houwwe, hiyye, henne, ne7na.

Possessive/Object clitics attach to nouns/preps/verbs: -e, -ak, -ik, -o, -a, -na, -kon, -on (poss.); -ni, -ak, -ik, -o, -a, -na, -kon, -on (obj.).

Preps + clitics: ma3e/ma3ak…, 3alayye/3alayk…, bi‑, la‑, menni/mennak…, 3ande/3andak…

6) Verb System

  • Present/Habitual: b‑ + base: brou7, b3mol.
  • Progressive: 3am + base: 3am rou7.
  • Past: perfective endings: re7et/ra7, 3melet/3mel.
  • Future: ra7/7a/la7 + base: ra7 rou7.
  • Imperative: 2nd person base: rou7!, kool!, eshreb!, 3mol!
  • Subjunctive-like base (drop b‑) after badd‑, lazem, moumken, la, ra7/7a/la7, 2abl ma, ba3d ma.
  • Negation: ma + (b‑)present, ma 3am + base, ma + past, mish (nominal), ma fi (existential), wala (not even).
  • Ability: fiini/fiik… or b2dar.

7) Questions, Word Order, Copula

  • Wh‑: shu, min, wen/wayn, eymta, kif, adde/addeish, leish. Yes/No: eh/la2.
  • Word order flexible (SVO/VSO). Copula omitted: ana mni7.
  • illi (relative), eno/anno (that… complementizer).

8) Numbers & Counting (Natural Patterns)

  • 0–20: sefer, wa7ad, tnen… 3eshrin.
  • 2 of X: -en/-ten; 3–10: number + plural; 11–19: number + singular; 20+: number + singular.

9) Comparison & Superlatives

  • Comparative: aktar + adj (works always). Many also use a7la, atwal…
  • Superlative: aktar shi + adj, often with context.
  • Equality: nafs, 2add.

10) Possession & Existence

  1. Idāfe: bayt Tony.
  2. taba3 + clitic: l sayyara taba3e.
  3. Have: 3and‑ + clitic; There is: fi / ma fi.

12) Politeness, Requests & Offers

  • Please: iza btireed/ti, law sama7t/ti.
  • Thanks: merci, shukran. Reply: ahlan, tikram/tekrami.
  • Blessing: y3tiik/i l 3afye. Reply: Allah y3afik/i.
  • Requests: bte2dar…?/fiik…? + base. Offers: btaakol shi? bteshrab shi?

13) Regional & Register Notes

  • qāf: 2 (urban) vs q (some rural) vs g (pockets).
  • mish is standard; mou exists but less common.
  • Loanwords from French/English are normal.

15) Syntax & Style Patterns

  • Purpose: la + base — ra7 rou7 la eshtre 5obez.
  • Before/after: 2abl ma / ba3d ma + base.
  • Because/so that: la2anno, mishan, 3ashen.
  • Conditionals: iza + present/base; result with b‑present or ra7.
  • Prohibitives: ma + base — ma t3ayyet!
  • Focus particles: bass, kamen, tab3an, ya3ne, heik.

16) Pitfalls

  1. Use b‑ (not bi‑) for present.
  2. Use mish to negate nouns/adjectives.
  3. Drop b‑ after badd‑/lazem/ra7…
  4. ma fi (there isn’t/aren’t), ma 3am (not doing now).
  5. Feminine -e and adjective agreement.
  6. Sun‑letter assimilation for l‑.
  7. Numbers: 3–10 plural; 11–19 singular; 20+ singular.

17) Practice Drills

  • Negation ladder: convert present → ma + b‑present → ma 3am + base.
  • Subjunctive triggers after badd‑/lazem/moumken/la/2abl ma/ba3d ma.
  • Clitic chains on verbs & preps.
  • Demonstratives near/far.
  • Counting with natural patterns.
